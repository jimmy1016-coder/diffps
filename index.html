Abstract

Person search aims to jointly perform person detection and person re-identification (re-ID) in a unified framework. Existing methods often suffer from optimization conflicts due to shared backbone usage. We propose DiffPS, which leverages pretrained diffusion models to extract rich visual features, freezes the backbone, and uses decoupled task-specific branches to fully exploit diffusion priors. This results in improved performance and stability across multiple person search benchmarks.

Method

DiffPS uses a frozen diffusion model backbone to provide rich spatial features, and separates task-specific heads for detection and Re-ID to avoid gradient interference. This decoupled design ensures stability and better representation learning.
